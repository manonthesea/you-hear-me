The Eleventh Hour

1 All week’s been waning and waxing,
2 gibbous and crescent
3 hidden to the naked eye until late.
4 Even then, without clear reflection, 
5 the night is claustrophobic white sheets 
6 at the end of a line.

7 Tomorrow, tonight’s not meant to mean a thing
8 if the wounded woman calls
9 at the top of the stairs, unrecognized; 
10 clouded response from thick-aired bedroom 
11 is a silent act, like visions of the night,
12 an indictment of wrongdoing.

13 At the eleventh hour, time's unstill.
14 Yesterday hides as today stares into space, 
15 and here lying still in the bedroom
16 that newfound bleached-white rock peers at me 
17 through tempered pane creating new meaning 
18 now recognized in proximity’s moonlight.

2.7.18
